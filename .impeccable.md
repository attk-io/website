## Users

Bootstrapped solo founders — typically non-technical, first-time or serial — at a specific inflection point: the product concept is clear, the market opportunity is real, they've done the groundwork (quit a job, put in personal capital, spent months or years on preparation), and the next step is building the thing. **The software is the business.** If the build fails, the venture fails.

They arrive at this site through a warm referral from someone in their network — not through ads, SEO, or cold outreach. They are not shopping around on Upwork. They are following a specific recommendation because they know they need someone good and they cannot independently evaluate "good."

Their context during the visit is a workday, usually on a desktop but sometimes a phone, often within hours of the referral conversation. They are reading alone, forming a gut judgment. The **job-to-be-done on this site** is narrow and high-stakes: decide whether to book a discovery call with Christian.

The two emotions they must feel by the time they close the tab are **trust** (primary) and **excitement** (secondary). They should finish reading and think: _"This feels honest. No games. I want to talk to this person."_

Anti-audience (the people this site should gently filter out): cost-shoppers, tire-kickers looking for the cheapest developer, people who want an agency with a formal process, and anyone comparing rates before value.

## Brand Personality

**Voice variant (lead):** Warm Authority — _knowledgeable, reassuring, personal._

The site should read like a letter from someone who has helped many founders through this exact moment and knows what they need to hear. Empathy comes first — name the fear of choosing the wrong builder, the frustration of not being able to evaluate technical quality — and then calmly address it with evidence. The directive: **lead with expertise.** The reader should feel they are in experienced hands, not emotionally held.

**Archetype:** Sage + Caregiver. Authority through demonstrated expertise and genuine care for the founder's outcome. Never asserted, never performed.

**Voice spectrums:**

- Formality: **Conversational** — contractions, natural rhythm, human pacing
- Energy: **Calm & understated** — authority through restraint, not volume
- Warmth: **Warm & personal** — openly empathetic

**Boundaries:**

- Confident, not arrogant
- Warm, not sycophantic — willing to tell the hard truth
- Calm, not passive — restraint is a choice, not a lack of conviction
- Empathetic, not patronizing
- Reassuring, not hand-holding
- Personal, not performative

**Copy anti-patterns** (from the brand strategy doc — never acceptable on this site):

- Generic agency-speak ("we build apps," "solutions")
- Jargon-heavy technical language
- Team-size or process-heavy framing
- "Move fast and break things" energy
- Price-first messaging
- Overpromising speed ("launch in 2 weeks")
- Startup-bro vocabulary (disrupt, 10x, crushing it)
- Stock photography of glass-office teams

## Aesthetic Direction

**Modern Swiss discipline.**

The visual language of single-typeface rigor — the lineage that runs from Akzidenz-Grotesk and Müller-Brockmann's grid systems through Vignelli's Helvetica-everything posters and into the modern interpretations at Linear, Vercel, and Rauno Freiberg's personal site. Hierarchy is achieved through weight, size, and discipline. Decoration earns no place.

**What that means concretely:**

- **Cool paper, dark ink.** Background is a near-white tinted subtly toward the highlight hue (`oklch(0.994 0.004 252)` — sand-1) so it never reads clinical-white but never reads warm either. Body ink is a near-black tinted toward the same hue (`sand-12`). The whole page reads cohesive without anyone noticing why.
- **Single-family typography.** One typeface — **Hanken Grotesk** — across every level: display H1, body, metadata labels, footnotes. Hierarchy comes from weight (300 / 400 / 500 / 600), size (~1.25 ratio between steps with fluid `clamp()` on display headings), and tracking discipline (negative tracking on display, neutral on body, positive on uppercase metadata labels). A `--font-spectral` token is preserved in `global.css` as a reference to an earlier serif exploration but is not loaded.
- **Display weight is semibold (600), not bold or black.** Bold reads as poster-loud; light reads as fashion-thin. Semibold is the confident-but-restrained Swiss register.
- **Tight tracking on display, tight leading.** Display H1/H2: tracking ~ -0.025 to -0.03em, leading ~ 0.98–1.05. Body: neutral tracking, leading 1.6.
- **Single disciplined column.** Narrow reading width (~65–72ch) for body sections. The body copy is the product. No cards, no tiles, no three-up feature grids.
- **Ink-dark highlight.** One highlight scale — **Ink** (deep fountain-pen blue-violet, OKLCH hue 252, step 9 at L 0.305) — used for links, the highlighter-style emphasis on a handful of words across the entire page, and the single primary CTA. Never as UI chrome, never as a panel background. Accents work because they are rare.
- **Sun and Teal as secondary tones.** Two additional Radix-style scales (`sun-1..sun-12`, `teal-1..teal-12`) live in the system, available where a section genuinely needs a second accent — status indicators, a secondary CTA, selective decoration. They never compete with Ink for primary attention. Both are visible on `/specimen` for reference.
- **Numbered hierarchy, hairline rules, uppercase labels with tracking.** Visible structure — phase numerals (01 · 02 · 03), uppercase metadata labels in Hanken Grotesk medium with letter-spacing 0.12–0.14em, hairline rules — earns its place by doing work. Faked small-caps via `uppercase + tracking` since the system has no dedicated small-caps family.
- **Whitespace as rhythm.** Varied spacing, not uniform padding. Some sections breathe generously; some sit tight. Rhythm is how hierarchy is expressed.
- **Theme: light only.** No dark mode. Cool near-white paper is load-bearing for the aesthetic.

**Primary references:**

- [Linear](https://linear.app) — single sans, ink-dark highlight, Swiss restraint at marketing scale
- [Vercel](https://vercel.com) — Geist family discipline, narrow content column thinking
- [Rauno Freiberg](https://rauno.me) — Swiss discipline at a personal scale
- Karl Gerstner's _Designing Programmes_ and Müller-Brockmann's _Grid Systems_ for the historical lineage

**Specimen page:** `/specimen` (in dev: `http://localhost:4322/specimen`). The specimen renders the live foundation system — type scale, all four color scales (sand, ink, sun, teal), highlight in context, and contrast notes. Treat it as a living style guide.

**Anti-references:**

- The archived `quiet-authority` direction (`archive/2026-04-10-quiet-authority/`). Do not borrow palette, typography, or layout patterns from it.
- The earlier "warm editorial precision" exploration (cream paper, rust accent, Spectral serif display) — abandoned in favor of Swiss.
- Generic developer / engineer portfolio tropes: monospace-everything, terminal aesthetics, "hacker personal site," dark VSCode backgrounds.
- Agency/SaaS marketing templates: hero-with-gradient, three-icon-card grids, glassmorphism, gradient text, testimonial carousels, CTA-heavy sections.
- Dark mode with glowing accents (cyan-on-dark, purple-to-blue gradients, neon).
- Distinctive display sans faces with strong character (Bricolage Grotesque, Cabinet Grotesk, etc.). They distract from the Swiss discipline. Hanken Grotesk does the whole job.
- Pure cold Swiss that loses every trace of humanity. The cool sand tint and the ink hue (rather than pure black) are where warmth lives.

**Accessibility target:** WCAG AA baseline. Body text ≥ 4.5:1, UI elements ≥ 3:1. All current foundation pairings exceed AA, and most exceed AAA (body text 17.6:1, low-contrast text 7.7:1, ink link color 10.9:1, ink button background 13.2:1). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` absolutely. Full keyboard reachability. Visible focus states that match the typographic language (sand-12 outline, never a default browser yellow ring).

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the statement.** The confidence of the voice is inseparable from the restraint of the design. Every element should feel like it was considered, then considered again for removal. When in doubt, take something away. Do not try to impress visually — try to disappear into the reading experience.

2. **Single-family discipline.** Authority comes from rigor, not variety. One typeface (Hanken Grotesk) used across every level of hierarchy with weight, size, and tracking as the only differentiators. No second display face. No serif/sans pairing. No quirks. The typographic system is the brand.

3. **Read it as a document, not a landing page.** Narrow single-column reading experience (~65–72ch). Body copy is the product, not a supporting element. Sections separated by rhythm and whitespace, not by cards or heavy dividers. No hero-metric boxes, no three-up feature grids, no testimonial carousels, no "as seen in" logo strips.

4. **Numbered hierarchy and hairline rules, never decoration.** Visible structure — phase numerals (01 · 02 · 03), uppercase metadata labels with letter-spacing, hairline horizontal rules — earns its place by doing work. No icon-above-heading card grids, no decorative borders, no flourishes.

5. **Cool neutrals, ink-dark highlight, sparing secondaries.** Cool near-white sand background. Cool near-black body ink, both subtly tinted toward the ink hue (252) for cohesion. **Ink** is the primary highlight, used rarely. **Sun** and **Teal** exist as secondary accents in the system but never compete with Ink for attention.

6. **Motion is rare and intentional.** A calm fade-and-translate reveal on first view, refined link hover states, a considered focus state. Nothing bounces, nothing shimmers, nothing parallaxes. `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored completely.

7. **Accessibility is part of precision.** WCAG AA baseline is non-negotiable. Contrast, focus, keyboard order, and reduced-motion aren't added at the end — they are part of the promise the design is making about the kind of work Christian does. The current system exceeds AAA on most pairings.
