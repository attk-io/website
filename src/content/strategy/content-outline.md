## Target Audience

### Qualifying Statement

A direct, confident statement that helps the right person self-identify.

Describe the ideal client's situation: what they already have (vision, domain expertise, drive) and what they're missing (technical execution, architecture, a builder they can trust). Make it clear this is a filter — it's okay if some people read this and realize it's not for them.

## Process / How It Works

Walk the reader through the engagement from first conversation to ongoing partnership. Each phase should set expectations for what happens, who does what, and what the client gets out of it.

### Phase 1: Discovery / Alignment

Mutual evaluation. Both sides figure out if there's a fit. Emphasize honesty and directness — no sales process, just a real conversation.

### Phase 2: Planning / Strategy

Translate the client's vision into a concrete, readable plan. Emphasize clarity — plain language, clear priorities, transparent trade-offs. The client should understand where their money goes.

### Phase 3: Execution / Build

The actual development work. Emphasize short cycles, visible progress, and clear communication. The client stays informed without needing to manage the technical work.

### Phase 4: Launch / Delivery

Ship a real, production-ready product — not a demo or prototype. Emphasize that this includes the unglamorous but critical work: infrastructure, deployment, monitoring.

### Phase 5: Iteration / Ongoing Support

Use real-world data and feedback to decide what comes next. Emphasize the collaborative dynamic — ATTK advises on what's technically possible, the client decides what matters for the business.

## About / Credentials

Establish credibility and trust. Introduce ATTK as a person (Christian Naths), not a faceless company. Emphasize: senior-level experience, single point of accountability, track record of shipping products that last. Address the unspoken fear — hiring the wrong builder is expensive.

## Pricing

Set the tone: be direct and transparent about costs. The goal is to remove ambiguity and build trust, not to sell.

### Tier 1 — Entry / Discovery Engagement

A low-commitment paid engagement to evaluate fit. The client gets value even if they don't proceed — a clear technical picture of what their product needs.

### Tier 2 — Foundation / Platform Build

The baseline infrastructure every product needs (auth, billing, hosting, etc.) but that isn't unique to the client's business. Give a price range and explain what drives the variance.

### Tier 3 — Ongoing / Custom Work

The custom business logic, features, and workflows that make the product unique. Scoped during discovery, billed hourly, built in short cycles with full visibility.
