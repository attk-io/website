## How It Works

### 1. Discovery

Before either of us commits, we need to know this is the right fit. You tell me about your product, your market, and what's at stake. I give you an honest assessment — not a sales pitch. If I don't think I'm the right person for your build, I'll tell you. If the scope doesn't make sense, I'll tell you that too. The worst thing I could do is let you invest in something I'm not confident I can deliver. This session is $1,500 — and if we don't move forward, you still walk away with a clear technical picture of what your product needs.

### 2. Plan

I translate your vision into a technical strategy you can actually read — plain language, not jargon. We decide together what gets built first, what waits, and what gets cut. That last part matters: I actively protect your project from scope creep. Every feature we add is a feature that has to earn its place. You'll see exactly where your money is going and why, with every trade-off made visible so you can make informed decisions about your own product.

### 3. Build

Development happens in short, focused cycles. Each week you'll see real progress and receive updates written for you, not for engineers — what was completed, what's next, and whether anything has changed. If something shifts, you'll hear about it from me directly, with context and a recommendation. You stay informed and in control of the strategic decisions. I handle the technical execution and make sure nothing falls through the cracks.

### 4. Launch

The goal is a production-ready product in real users' hands — not a demo, not a prototype. I handle everything that keeps it running: infrastructure, deployment, monitoring, security. The work that nobody sees but that determines whether your product survives contact with the real world. You shouldn't have to worry about whether the foundation is solid. That's my job.

### 5. Iterate

Once the product is live, we have something more valuable than assumptions — we have real data. Together, we look at what's working, what isn't, and where the best opportunities are. I'll tell you what's technically feasible and what I'd recommend. You decide what matters most for your business. The partnership doesn't end at launch — it evolves based on what the market actually tells us.

## FAQ

**What if the project doesn't work out?**
That's exactly what discovery is designed to prevent. By the time we commit to a full build, we've both had a thorough look at what we're building and whether the partnership makes sense. I don't take on projects I'm not confident I can deliver — and I'd rather be honest about that up front than let you find out after you've invested months and money.

**How involved do I need to be?**
You stay in control of the decisions that shape your business — priorities, direction, trade-offs. I own the technical execution entirely and keep you informed in plain language, not jargon. The goal is for you to always know what's happening with your product without needing to manage the build yourself. It's your company — you should never feel like you've lost visibility into how it's being built.

**How is this different from hiring an agency?**
At an agency, your project is one of many. Communication passes through account managers, work gets distributed across teams you've never met, and no single person is accountable for the whole. Here, it's just me — one person who is personally responsible for your product from start to finish. No layers, no hand-offs, no wondering who's actually doing the work.

**Can you handle a larger project?**
Yes. I've hired and managed teams of developers when the scope required it. The model scales, but the accountability doesn't change — I remain the single point of contact and the person ultimately responsible for what gets shipped. Your project doesn't get passed around.

**What happens when something goes wrong?**
I tell you. Directly, in plain language, with a clear plan for how we fix it. You will never be blindsided by a problem I saw coming. Issues are inevitable in any build — what matters is that someone is watching for them and dealing with them honestly. That's what I'm here for.

**Who owns the code?**
You do. Everything I build — the codebase, the infrastructure, the documentation — belongs to you completely. If we ever part ways, you'll have a well-organized, well-documented product that any competent developer can pick up and continue building. You're never locked in. Your product is yours.
