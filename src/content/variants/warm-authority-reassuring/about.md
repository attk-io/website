## About

My name is Christian Naths. I'm a senior software engineer based in Canada, and for the past decade I've been the person founders call when they need someone experienced enough to take full ownership of their technical build.

I've seen what works and what doesn't. I've watched projects succeed because the right things were prioritized at the right time, and I've seen others struggle because no one with enough experience was in the room when the early decisions were made. That experience shapes everything about how I work — from the questions I ask during discovery to the way I structure a build to the moments I push back on scope that would hurt the product.

The person you talk to is the person who builds. There are no layers, no hand-offs, no junior developers learning on your project. When I take something on, I'm accountable for the whole — architecture, design, development, deployment. The products I've built are still running in production years after launch. One was acquired, and I handled the technical due diligence personally. They've lasted because they were built by someone who knew what durability required.

When a project requires more than one person, I hire and manage additional developers. When it doesn't, you have my complete focus. In either case, the experience and judgment stay the same.
