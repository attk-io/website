# Direction & Tone Variations

> Strategic reference document for ATTK Software website copy.
> Derived from a brand voice discovery process using archetype selection, voice spectrum mapping, boundary-setting, and emotional targeting.
> Companion to: [target-audience.md](target-audience.md)

---

## Voice Center

The voice center is the anchor point all variations share. Each variant pushes in a different direction while staying tethered to these fundamentals.

### Archetype

**Sage** (primary) + **Caregiver** (secondary)

The trusted expert who genuinely cares about the founder's outcome. Expertise is demonstrated through the work and through clear, honest communication — never asserted or performed. The caregiver element means the voice always considers what the founder is feeling and what they need to hear, not just what's technically accurate.

### Voice Spectrums

| Dimension | Position                                                                                         |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Formality | **Conversational** — like talking to a smart friend. Contractions, natural rhythm, human pacing. |
| Energy    | **Calm & understated** — authority through restraint, not volume. Lets the work speak.           |
| Warmth    | **Warm & personal** — openly empathetic. Acknowledges the founder's fears and excitement.        |

### Boundaries

- **Confident, not arrogant** — We know we're good, but we don't need to prove it by tearing down others or overselling ourselves.
- **Warm, not sycophantic** — We genuinely care about the founder's success, but we're not people-pleasers. We'll tell you the hard truth.
- **Calm, not passive** — We're understated, but there's real conviction behind what we say. Restraint is a choice, not a lack of opinion.

### Target Emotions

After reading the ATTK website, the ideal prospect should feel:

1. **Excitement** — "This person could actually make my vision real. I want to talk to them."
2. **Trust** — "This feels honest and real. No games, no upsell. I believe this person."

## Selected Variant

For ATTK website copy, **Quiet Confidence** is the primary voice. When in doubt, write toward restraint: let the work and the structure do the convincing, and trust the reader to recognize quality without being told.

The other variants below remain useful as counterpoints during copy exploration, or for specific surfaces that call for a different lean (e.g., a higher-energy CTA, or a section that needs to name the founder's anxiety directly). But the default — and the voice the rest of the site should harmonize with — is Quiet Confidence.

## Variations

Tone descriptors are listed in order of dominance — the first is the primary voice quality, the rest are supporting. The **Lead with** directive is the single most important instruction when drafting copy for a variant: when in doubt, follow it.

### 1. Quiet Confidence

**Tone:** Assured, understated, descriptive

**Lead with:** Restraint — let the track record do the convincing. When in doubt, say less.

Lets the work speak for itself. Calm, fluid prose that paints a clear picture without overselling. Authority comes from restraint and track record, not from assertion. Descriptive enough to feel warm and grounded, restrained enough to never feel like a pitch. This variant trusts the reader to recognize quality — it doesn't point at itself. The excitement it generates is quiet: the reader finishes and thinks "this is clearly the real thing."

**Example headline:**

> I build software for founders who can't afford to get it wrong.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Calm authority, not detachment
- Descriptive, not flowery
- Restrained, not timid

---

### 2. Straight Talk

**Tone:** Direct, plainspoken, conversational

**Lead with:** Clarity — every sentence should make the reader feel like the fog just lifted.

Says exactly what it means with zero filler. Short sentences, clear claims, no hedging. This is the variant that feels most like an actual conversation — the way Christian would explain what he does over coffee. It builds trust through radical transparency: here's what I do, here's what it costs, here's what you get. The excitement comes from clarity itself — the founder finally encounters someone who speaks their language instead of hiding behind jargon or process.

**Example headline:**

> You need software built. I build software. Let's figure out if we're a fit.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Direct, not blunt
- Simple, not simplistic
- Honest, not harsh

---

### 3. Warm Authority

**Tone:** Knowledgeable, reassuring, personal

**Lead with:** Expertise — the reader should feel they're in experienced hands, not emotionally held.

Leads with empathy and backs it with expertise. This variant names what the founder is feeling — the anxiety of choosing the wrong builder, the frustration of not being able to evaluate technical quality — and then calmly addresses it. It reads like a letter from someone who has helped many founders through this exact moment and knows exactly what they need to hear. Trust is the primary emotion; excitement follows from feeling genuinely understood.

**Example headline:**

> You've been carrying this vision alone. Let me take the technical weight off your shoulders.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Empathetic, not patronizing
- Reassuring, not hand-holding
- Personal, not performative

---

### 4. Builder's Pride

**Tone:** Craft-forward, passionate, grounded

**Lead with:** Care for the work — the reader should sense someone who builds things to last, not someone selling themselves.

Centers the love of building and the quality of what gets made. This variant cares deeply about the work itself — the architecture, the durability, the details that most people never see. It speaks to founders who want to know their product is built by someone who takes genuine pride in their craft, not just someone fulfilling a contract. The excitement comes from sensing that this person _loves_ what they do; trust comes from the obvious depth of care.

**Example headline:**

> Every product I build is one I'd put my name on. Yours will be no different.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Proud of the craft, not self-congratulatory
- Detail-oriented, not precious
- Passionate, not intense

---

### 5. Founder's Ally

**Tone:** Protective, partnership-oriented, empathetic

**Lead with:** Protection — the reader should feel shielded from risk, not emotionally held.

Frames everything from the founder's perspective. This variant sees the engagement through the founder's eyes — their risks, their stakes, their fears about choosing wrong. It positions ATTK not as a vendor or even an expert, but as someone who is genuinely on the founder's side. The protective quality is distinct: this voice will tell you when to cut scope, when to say no, when to ship. Trust is built by making the founder feel like ATTK's success is inseparable from theirs; excitement comes from the sense of having a real ally.

**Example headline:**

> Your product is too important to hand off to strangers. Let's build it together.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Protective, not possessive
- Partnership-oriented, not dependent
- Founder-first, not self-effacing

## Departures

These variants push beyond the voice center's calm & understated energy. They share the same archetype foundation (Sage + Caregiver) and boundaries, but dial up the intensity, directness, or provocation. Useful as counterpoints during copy exploration — they test how far the voice can stretch before it stops feeling like ATTK.

### 6. Punchy Partnership

**Tone:** Punchy, direct, forward-leaning

**Lead with:** Momentum — the reader should feel like things are already moving.

**Source:** [strategic-partnership-punchy-direct.md](../strategic-partnership-punchy-direct.md)

A higher-energy take on the Founder's Ally direction. Blends partnership framing with Straight Talk directness — short declarative sentences, no hedging, a pace that pulls you forward. The warmth is still there but it's expressed through candor rather than reassurance. Where Quiet Confidence trusts the reader to get it, this variant grabs them by the collar and says "here's how it is."

**Example headline:**

> Your vision + my track record = your competitive edge.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Punchy, not aggressive
- Confident, not salesy
- Fast-paced, not breathless

---

### 7. Challenger

**Tone:** Confrontational, provocative, direct

**Lead with:** Contrast — name what's broken first, then present the alternative.

**Source:** [challenger-confrontational-direct.md](../challenger-confrontational-direct.md)

The furthest departure from the voice center. Leads by calling out what's broken — agencies, dev shops, outsourcing — and positions ATTK as the antidote. Assumes the reader has already been burned or is frustrated with the status quo. This variant has real edge: it names the problem before offering the solution. It builds trust through shared frustration rather than empathy, and generates excitement through the promise of something fundamentally different.

**Example headline:**

> Stop outsourcing to agencies. Start working with a builder.

**This variant is / is not:**

- Provocative, not hostile
- Opinionated, not dismissive
- Contrarian, not cynical
