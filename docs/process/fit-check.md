# Fit Check

This document captures how the entry-point conversation runs — the free exchange that decides whether a paid Discovery engagement starts. Plain wording — no brand voice applied. Internal planning doc.

The Fit Check is the only stage of the engagement that's free. No code, no scoping, no commitment beyond the conversation itself. Its job is to produce a clear go / no-go decision in as little time as the question honestly takes.

## Format

- A single ~30-minute video call is the default. One conversation is usually enough; occasionally a short follow-up is needed before either side can commit.
- Async (email or shared doc) works when scheduling is hard, time zones don't line up, or the founder thinks better in writing. The same questions get answered; the deliverable is the same.
- If a synchronous call has happened, I write up a short summary afterward and send it across, so both sides have the same record of what was discussed and decided.
- Free. No NDA required by default — if the founder needs one, a lightweight mutual NDA can be signed before the call.

## Time commitment

- Founder: ~30 minutes for the call itself, plus the time to think through the intake questions ahead of it (usually under an hour).
- Me: ~30 minutes for the call, ~15 minutes prep reading whatever the founder has sent, ~15 minutes writing up the outcome.
- Total elapsed time from first contact to decision: typically under a week.

## Intake — what gets asked

The founder doesn't need to have polished answers. The questions are meant to surface what's known and what isn't.

- **What are you building?** A paragraph in their own words, plus any existing artifacts — deck, doc, sketches, prototype, an existing site.
- **What stage are you at?** Idea, validated problem, paying pilots, existing product needing a rebuild, etc.
- **Why now?** What's making this the right moment to start — funding, market window, personal runway, an event.
- **Who is it for?** The founder's current read on the target user and the problem being solved.
- **What's the timeline you're working toward?** Hard deadlines (event, funding milestone, contract), soft targets, or "as fast as is sensible."
- **What do you think you need from me right now?** v0? v1-beta? Discovery only? Advisory? Their framing is useful even when it turns out to be wrong.
- **What's the rough budget shape?** Order of magnitude is enough — ten thousand, fifty thousand, six figures. Catches mismatches early. - **What have you tried already?** Other developers, agencies, no-code, in-house attempts. What worked, what didn't, why we're talking.
- **Why this kind of engagement?** What's drawing them to a solo technical partner over hiring, an agency, or a co-founder search.

## Two-way fit — what each side is checking

**What I'm checking for:**

- Is this a product I can do good work on — do I have, or can I reasonably build, the context this domain needs?
- Is the founder the kind of partner the engagement model assumes — clear thinking, decisive enough to sign off on scope and QA, available for the cadence the development cycle requires?
- Is the timeline realistic against the scope they're imagining, or is one of them going to have to give?
- Is the budget shape compatible with the engagement model (Discovery → Base Platform → Custom Work)?
- Is the problem one I find interesting enough to commit months or years to — the engagement isn't short, and conviction matters.

**What the founder is checking for:**

- Am I a credible technical partner for what they're building.
- Do I understand their world well enough to be useful from the start.
- Is the engagement model — solo, paid Discovery up front, set-by-set development — actually what they want.
- Is there a basis for trust over a multi-month relationship.

## Outcomes

**If it's a fit:**

- Agreement to begin a paid Discovery engagement.
- A rough time budget for Discovery (two to three working days) and a target start window.
- An understanding of the broad shape of the project, enough for Discovery to be set up usefully.
- A short written follow-up confirming the above, sent within a day or two of the call.

**If it's not a fit:**

- An honest read on why — wrong stage, wrong domain, timeline / budget mismatch, model mismatch, or a problem I'm not the right person for.
- Where possible, a pointer to a more appropriate option: a different kind of partner, a no-code path, an agency, a hire profile, or a different sequencing of the work.
- The founder leaves with a clearer articulation of what they're building, even if the answer is "not with me" — the intake questions tend to be useful regardless of the outcome.

## Dependencies and what follows

- **Depends on:** nothing — this is the entry point.
- **Feeds into:** the Discovery milestone (see `project-arc.md`). The Fit Check sets the time budget for Discovery, confirms two-way fit, and establishes the broad shape of the project that Discovery then makes granular.
